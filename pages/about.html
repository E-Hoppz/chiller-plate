What is Remedy?

How We Started and What We're All About

We Want a Safe Place For You


Everybody has bad days, the days where we face stress, sadness, sorrow, and heartache. But, everybody deserves to be happy, and we all deserve to find our joy, and to find that safe space where we can just be ourselves.

Remedy Health was founded to uplift, heal, and guide its users on a journey to discover their joy. In today’s rapidly changing world, happiness is difficult to discover, and many struggle with their mental health. Here, at Remedy Health, we believe that everybody should have a place where they can be joyous and carefree. A place where they are safe. It is our goal to provide that safe space for you to find your joy and to find the tips that you need to thrive.

Remedy provides an environment where anyone can find tranquility and gladness by providing calming mindfulness exercises, inspiring and emotional quotes, movie suggestions, and playlists pertaining to specific moods and experiences, which are all tailored to your preferences. Remedy is happiness made easy.

The founders of Remedy are five teenagers who attended Nalukai Academy, a business and entrepreneur camp located in Hawaiʻi, who decided that there should be a way for individuals to find their happy place. Instead of spending time mindlessly sifting through Instagram, Snapchat, Facebook, and various other forms of social media rather, phone users should have the option to scroll through something meaningful and up lifting.

THE FOUNDERS: A LITTLE ABOUT US

  • Meenakshi Kutty is a sophomore at St. Andrew's Schools- The Priory. Ever since a very young age, she's been intrested in the arts and wants to pursue her dreams in some medium of art. She's also very passionate about all that she has at Nalukai and with Remedy. She would like to thank her facilitators and friends at Nalukai for giving her the best opportunity a student and teenager could ever have.
  • Allena Villanueva is a sophomore at Punahou School. She enjoys robotics, playing and listening to music, sleeping, and learning about different cultures. Nalukai changed her life, much for the better.
  • Falyn Sebastian is a graduate of Honoka'a High School on the Big Island. She will be attending Colorado State University in the Fall of 2019 and is majoring in Graphic Design. She holds a passion for the arts and making people feel loved.
  • Elisha Stutes is a former student of Christian Liberty Academy, who resides on the Big Island. He is a programmer in training seeking to help the world and solve problems using hi skills in technology.
  • Andrew Dawson is a junior at 'Iolani School on the island of O'ahu. He loves to play basketball and water polo, watching movies hanging out with his friends, and helping others. He wants to major in Engineering while continuing to pursue his dream of playing college sports.
